# dmp-web

## Design Context

### Users
IT administrators and small/mid IT teams evaluating a **self-hosted** device-management
platform. They land here from a repo link or word of mouth, on desktop, deciding "is
this worth installing on our network?" They value control, privacy (no cloud), and
clarity over hype.

### Brand Personality
Calm · clinical · precise. Confident and quiet — an instrument, not a billboard. The
voice is plain and technical; it respects the reader's competence.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first "Clinical Petrol" / **instrument-panel** identity, continuous with the app.
Deep petrol-navy surfaces (never pure black), petrol-teal primary, **mint-teal** as the
rare 10% accent, amber used once or twice. References: obsproject.com, github.com/features.
Anti-references: generic purple-gradient SaaS landings, glassmorphism, neon-on-dark.

Signature detail (taken to 120%): a **monospace "asset-tag" label** motif — the page
labels its own screenshots like tracked assets (`DMP-DASH-01`, `v1.0`, `⌘K`).
Centerpiece: a **3D hover-tilt** screenshot showcase.

### Design Principles
1. **Show the product.** Real screenshots are the hero; copy supports them, never the reverse.
2. **Instrument, not billboard.** Precision, hairline borders, monospace data chips — restraint over decoration.
3. **One accent, used rarely.** Mint-teal earns attention because it's scarce (60-30-10).
4. **Motion with intent.** One orchestrated load + tactile hover-tilt; no scattered micro-animations; honor reduced-motion.
5. **No AI tells.** No gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no emoji icons, no SVG-drawn imagery.

### Type
Display: Schibsted Grotesk · Body: Public Sans · Data/mono: Geist Mono.
